Activation steering · variance hierarchy

"Bank" has two meanings.One is loud. One is quiet.

A polysemous word lives as two clusters in activation space — financial institution and river edge. But the institution sense dominates the variance, so contrastive steering and top-component methods lock onto it and never see the river sense. This page shows the river meaning sitting in PC1, a non-dominant direction, and how looking past PC0 recovers it. All figures are computed live from the points.

01 / THE PICTURE IN ACTIVATION SPACE

Two senses of "bank", plus a contrast word

Each dot is a toy d=2 activation. The negative / contrast class is a different word ("stone") — it's only the baseline that contrastive steering subtracts, not a meaning of bank. The two senses of "bank" are institution (the common sense, far from the baseline) and river (rarer, offset in a different direction).

02 / WHAT STANDARD METHODS RECOVER

One loud direction → only the institution sense

Both classic moves collapse "bank" to a single direction. Diff-of-means subtracts the contrast word: mean(bank) − mean(stone). Top-PC takes the loudest axis, PC0. Either way the river sense — a minority offset — gets averaged away. The arrow lands on institution; river is unreachable.

03 / WHERE THE RIVER SENSE WENT

It's in PC1 — the quiet component

Run PCA on the activations and read the spectrum. PC0 holds most of the variance: it's the institution-vs-baseline axis. PC1 is small — but it is exactly where the river sense lives. Project the points onto each axis to see it: on PC0 the two senses overlap; on PC1 the river sense separates out.

04 / RECOVERING THE SECOND MEANING

Suppress PC0, cluster the residual, build both vectors

Project PC0 out: H − H·v₀v₀ᵀ. The institution-vs-baseline variance vanishes and the residual is just PC1, where the river cluster stands alone. Cluster it, and now there are two steering directions — institution along PC0, river from the recovered cluster. Both senses become reachable.

05 / THE GENERAL POINT

A concept needn't be the top direction of variation

ApproachDirection it usesRecovers
Diff-of-meansmean(bank) − mean(stone)Institution only

Supervised probeany predictive directionDetects river exists — but not how to steer it
Variance hierarchyPC0 and PC1 (after suppression)Both senses

Unsupervised methods follow the dominant axis. If a target meaning has lower variance than another, it hides beneath the loud one. Reading the whole spectrum — and suppressing the dominant component to expose the residual — is what surfaces the quiet meaning.
