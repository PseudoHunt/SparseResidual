Interactive figure · contrastive vs. region-aware steering

Diff-of-means averagesa meaning out of existence.

The classic steering vector is d = mean(positive) − mean(negative). When the positive class holds two meanings of "bank" — a financial institution and a bank account — one global vector points at their average and reaches whichever is bigger, silently dropping the smaller one. This page builds that exact situation and shows the variance-hierarchy method recovering both regions and steering each on its own. Everything is computed live from the points.

01 / THE CONTRASTIVE SETUP

One negative class, a positive class with two meanings

Each point is a toy d=2 activation at the "bank" token. The negative / contrast class is the river sense. The positive class is the financial sense — but it lives in two separated activation regions: institution contexts (the majority) and account contexts (the minority). Diff-of-means only ever sees "positive vs negative," so it collapses both financial regions into a single mean.

03 / HOW THE METHOD KNOWS TO LOOK

When the meaning hides under a nuisance axis

Diff-of-means at least uses labels. Unsupervised clustering follows the loudest axis — so if token-position variance is louder than the sense, naive k-means recovers position, not meaning. The method runs the variance hierarchy, flags the top axis when it correlates with token_position, suppresses it, then clusters the residual. Slide the nuisance up to cross from Dominant into Masked.
